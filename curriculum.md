# Music Education Curriculum

## Track 1: Theory Fundamentals

### Module 1: Sound & Pitch
- [ ] **1.1** What is sound? (frequency, amplitude, timbre)
- [ ] **1.2** The 12 notes and the octave
- [ ] **1.3** Sharps, flats, and enharmonic equivalents

### Module 2: Scales & Keys
- [ ] **2.1** The major scale (whole/half step pattern)
- [ ] **2.2** The natural minor scale
- [ ] **2.3** Keys and key signatures — what "in the key of C" means
- [ ] **2.4** Relative major/minor relationships

### Module 3: Intervals
- [ ] **3.1** What intervals are and why they matter
- [ ] **3.2** The intervals within an octave (unison through octave)
- [ ] **3.3** Interval quality — perfect, major, minor, augmented, diminished
- [ ] **3.4** Hearing intervals — associating each with familiar melodies

### Module 4: Chords
- [ ] **4.1** Triads — building chords from scales
- [ ] **4.2** Major, minor, diminished, augmented triads
- [ ] **4.3** Seventh chords and extensions (intro level)
- [ ] **4.4** Chord inversions

### Module 5: Rhythm & Meter
- [ ] **5.1** Beat, tempo, and BPM
- [ ] **5.2** Note durations (whole, half, quarter, eighth, sixteenth)
- [ ] **5.3** Time signatures (4/4, 3/4, 6/8)
- [ ] **5.4** Syncopation and swing

### Module 6: Harmony & Progressions
- [ ] **6.1** Diatonic chords — the chords that belong to a key
- [ ] **6.2** Roman numeral notation (I, ii, iii, IV, V, vi, vii°)
- [ ] **6.3** Common progressions (I-IV-V, I-V-vi-IV, ii-V-I, 12-bar blues)
- [ ] **6.4** Tension and resolution — dominant to tonic

### Module 7: Melody
- [ ] **7.1** What makes a melody memorable
- [ ] **7.2** Melodic contour and phrasing
- [ ] **7.3** Relationship between melody and harmony

### Module 8: Song Structure & Arrangement
- [ ] **8.1** Common song forms (verse-chorus, AABA, 12-bar)
- [ ] **8.2** Dynamics and texture — building and releasing energy
- [ ] **8.3** Instrumentation and layering
- [ ] **8.4** Connecting it all — analyzing a real song

## Track 2: Ear Training (parallel exercises)
- [ ] **E1** Recognizing octaves and unisons
- [ ] **E2** Identifying major vs. minor (scales and chords)
- [ ] **E3** Interval recognition (ascending)
- [ ] **E4** Interval recognition (descending)
- [ ] **E5** Chord quality identification
- [ ] **E6** Identifying instruments by timbre
- [ ] **E7** Rhythm pattern dictation
- [ ] **E8** Key identification by ear
